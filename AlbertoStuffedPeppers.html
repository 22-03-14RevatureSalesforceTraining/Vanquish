Stuffed Peppers

The Stuffed Pepper is common cuisine that has had many variations over the years. Although most variations consist of a hollowed out pepper filled with meat, rice, and cheese, there have been even more creative recipes throughout the world. In Hong Kong the recipe comes with filled sausages and is known as the "Three Great Treasures". In spain the recipe usually comes with cod covered in a red sauce. For our recipe today we will be doing something a bit more traditional.

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit
  2. Cut top of pepper off. Remove seeds and membranes. In oven add enough water to cover peppers and heat to boiling. Cook for about 2 minutes then drain the water.
  3. In skillet cook beef and onion over medium heat for 8 to 10 minutes. Stir in rice, salt, garlic and 1 cup of the tomato sauce. Cook until hot.
  4. Stuff peppers with beef mixture. Stand peppers upright in square glass baking dish. Pour remaining tomato sauce over peppers.
  5. Cover tightly with foil, bake 10 minutes. Uncover and bake 15 minutes longer. Sprinkle with cheese and serve when tender.
